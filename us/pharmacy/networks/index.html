How we partnered with NCAP
to launch statewide medical billing.

When the North Carolina Association of Pharmacists wanted to help member pharmacies build sustainable revenue beyond dispensing, they partnered with MedMe to run a four-month medical billing pilot, protocol-aligned, member-priced, and built around the workflow their pharmacies actually run. This is the partnership playbook we run with banners, co-ops, and PSAOs.

The pilot process, step by step

01 Pre-launch

Onboarding

Payer review, billing setup, EDI enrollment, and finalized go-live date. Workflow configuration shaped around the statewide protocols. Two to four weeks.

02 Month 1

Go-live and ramp-up

Pharmacies launch services and begin delivering appointments through MedMe workflows. Scheduler, intake, documentation, and billing all live from day one.

03 Months 2 and 3

Evaluation and optimization

Track utilization, claim submission performance, and pharmacy feedback. Optimize documentation and billing rules to improve results across the cohort.

04 Month 4

Outcomes and scaling

Consolidate outcomes and ROI insights, share back with NCAP, and align on next steps for scaling services and onboarding additional sites across the network.

The same partnership pattern, shared protocols, member-priced billing, and a structured pilot path from go-live to scale, is how we work with banners, co-ops, and PSAOs across North America.
