About MedMe Health

Pharmacy is the most under-used
care setting in the US.

We're building the platform that fixes it. Pharmacy-specific EHR, AI-drafted SOAP notes, and provincial billing billing — so the work pharmacists already do finally gets reimbursed.

Our mission

Help pharmacy do the clinical
work it's paid to do.

The Canadian pharmacy workforce already does counseling, immunization administration, chronic-care follow-up, transitions-of-care, and adherence monitoring. Less than 20% of it gets billed.

The reason is not clinical capability. It's documentation, coding, eligibility, and claim infrastructure built for medical groups — not for community pharmacy.

MedMe ships the missing layer: an EHR pharmacists actually want to use, an AI scribe that drafts the note, and a billing engine that converts the encounter into a clean claim. One platform, no swivel-chair.

Our journey

Six years building
for pharmacy.

2020

Founded

Started in Toronto by Nick Hui, Rui Su, and Purya Sarmadi after the COVID immunization rollout exposed how little clinical software pharmacy actually had.

2022

EHR launches

Full pharmacy EHR + scheduling deployed across 4,500+ Canadian pharmacies. The first platform built end-to-end for clinical pharmacy services.

2024

US expansion

AI Scribe and Medical Billing launch for Canadian community pharmacy. First 100 stores go live. $48M in clinical reimbursement processed in year one.

2026

Today

12M+ patients managed, 4,500+ Canadian pharmacies enrolled. Now serving independents, regional chains, top-100 systems, and payvider partners.

What we believe

Pharmacy-shaped opinions
we ship into the product.

Workflow first, features second

The pharmacist's hour is the most expensive resource in the building. Every feature pays its rent in minutes saved per encounter, or it doesn't ship.

Clinical scope is a revenue strategy

Reimbursement codes exist for the services pharmacy is already delivering. The job is to make billing trivial — not to convince pharmacists to do new clinical work.

Independents matter

Most clinical pharmacy platforms target chains. We start with the 60% of US retail pharmacy that's independent — because that's where community care actually happens.

Operators set the roadmap

Every quarter, top-decile customer operators tell us what's broken. We ship against their list, not ours. Our PRDs come from the field, not the conference room.
