Where You Are On The Curve

Four stages of enterprise
clinical-services maturity.

Every enterprise pharmacy we work with sits somewhere on a clinical-services maturity curve, from a still-mostly-dispensing operation to a multi-state, payer-contracted clinical operation. The modules you adopt, the rollout pattern, and the commercial structure all hinge on where you are today and where you want to be next.

Stage 01

Foundation

Vaccines, dispensing-led

Clinical services are limited to immunizations and the occasional MTM. Scheduling is on the dispensing system. Billing is largely cash and PBM-routed. The opportunity is to start running clinical services as a distinct revenue line.

Stage 02

Emerging

First clinical services, ad hoc

Some stores have started MTM and POCT. Documentation lives in spreadsheets or the dispensing add-on. Billing runs through one MAC region. The opportunity is to standardize the workflow and turn what is working into a program.

Stage 03

Established

Full clinical program live

CCM, MTM, POCT, vaccines, and incident-to billing are running. There is a dedicated clinical-services lead. Documentation discipline is consistent across stores. The opportunity is to scale across states and contract directly with payers.

Stage 04

Advanced

Multi-state, payer-contracted

Clinical services run as a network-wide line of business. Multi-MAC billing, direct payer contracts, and structured outcomes reporting are in place. The opportunity is interoperability, RHTP participation, and provider-status pathways.

Interoperability and Future-Forward

is becoming next.

Interoperability is not a settled requirement for most enterprises yet. For the ones thinking ahead, MedMe is already deployed as an interoperable pharmacy EHR, federated through QHIN, and in active early-state implementations of the federal Rural Health Transformation Program.

QHIN

QHIN-integrated

Federated medication history retrieval at point of care, through a Qualified Health Information Network exchange. The pharmacist sees the patient's full medication picture, not just what your PMS knows.

RHTP

Rollout Playbook

Three rollout patterns,
tuned to how your network actually moves.

We have rolled out clinical services platforms across enterprise pharmacy networks in three distinct ways, from a single-state pilot to a thousand-store cutover in a single weekend. Pick the pattern that fits your change-management appetite.

Single-state pilot

Lowest change-management lift

Stand up MedMe in one state or one operating region first. Validate the clinical workflow, the billing posture, and the patient-facing experience before any wider rollout decision gets made.

Phased state-by-state

Steady rollout, predictable load

Sequence the rollout state by state, region by region. Each wave benefits from the playbook the previous wave wrote. Operations, training, and clinical configuration move in lockstep with your team.

Big-bang network cutover

All-network at once

For the networks that want one date on the calendar: every store on the new platform on the same weekend. We have delivered this across networks of more than one thousand locations.
