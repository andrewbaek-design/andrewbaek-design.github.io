Core Scheduling Features

Pharmacy-specific scheduling,
end to end.

Every feature is designed around how pharmacy clinical services actually run, from a single-store immunization clinic to a 40-store network coordinating walk-ins, appointments, rooms, and external clinics at the same time.

Multi-Calendar

Multi-calendar views and time blocks

Coordinate every detail of your pharmacy's operations with a versatile multi-calendar system. Configure schedules for credentialed professionals, allocate consultation rooms, and block times to control when patients can book.

Multi-Service

Multi-service booking

One booking can cover multiple services in the same visit. Patients pick the combinations your pharmacy offers, the scheduler routes the booking to the right pharmacist and room, and your team works the visit as one encounter.

External Clinics

Beyond the pharmacy walls

Take services to workplaces, schools, community centers, and pop-up clinics. MedMe supports the setup and management of external clinics, with centralized scheduling across every location your pharmacists serve.

Rooms & Resources

Rooms, queues, and resources

Allocate consultation rooms, manage walk-in queues, and balance load across pharmacists in the same view. The scheduler enforces the rules your operations require, so the right service ends up in the right room with the right credentialed professional.

Reserve with Google

Get found and booked from Google search.

Reserve with Google integration puts your pharmacy's services in front of patients searching nearby. They tap the booking link straight from search results and land in the same MedMe scheduler your team already runs, so every walk-up patient becomes a booked appointment without a phone call.

Hybrid Walk-In and Appointment Flow

Walk-ups and bookings,
handled in the same view.

Pharmacy traffic is never purely walk-in or purely booked. The scheduler handles both in one queue, with automated reminders and recall outreach keeping the booked side full and your team's day predictable.

Walk-ins and appointments together

The queue accepts walk-ups and booked patients in the same view. Pharmacists see who is in the store now, what they came in for, and what's coming up later in the day.

Automated reminders and notifications

SMS and email reminders go out on the cadence each service needs. Patients confirm or reschedule by replying. No-shows drop without manual follow-up calls.

Recall outreach that books

Bring back patients due for CCM, MTM, A1C, or seasonal vaccines through SMS or email campaigns. Replies route directly into the scheduler, so a single campaign turns into a queue of booked follow-ups.
